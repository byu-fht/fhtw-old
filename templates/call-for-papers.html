{% filter markdown %} ## Scope of the Workshop The 2016 Family History Technology Workshop will bring together developers, researchers, technology professionals, and users to discuss the future of family history technology and genealogical research. The workshop will feature developer sessions, lightning talks, technical presentations, panels, and demos to showcase emerging and future technologies. ## Lightning Talk: What's Your Problem? This session will be oriented toward users helping developers and researchers understand the problems they are facing. Give a 2 to 5 minute presentation on a family history problem that you think technology can solve. What is your problem? Who does this problem affect? What impact would solving this problem have on family history? Do you have an idea for how technology could fix this? To submit an idea for a lightning talk, send us a one paragraph description of what you will cover. Include a short biographical statement explaining your background with family history. ## Lightning Talk: What's Your Tech? This session will be oriented toward developers showcasing the latest work they have done. Give a 2 to 5 minute presentation on your latest work in family history technology. We welcome startups, new open source projects, work-in-progress, or strongly-held (but informative) opinions on where the future of family history technology will go. To submit an idea for a lightning talk, send us a one paragraph description of what you will cover. Include a short biographical statement and a link to your product or web site and to your social media profile. Indicate whether you would also like to provide a demo in between sessions. ## Developer Talk Give a 10 to 15 minute talk about new tools, libraries, or languages, accompanied by a demo of what is being developed. Our emphasis is on novel approaches that will impact the future of family history technology. To submit an idea for a developer talk, send us a 1 - 2 paragraph description of what you will cover. Include a short biographical statement and a link to your developer profile on GitHub or other relevant sites. ## Research Talk Give a 10 to 15 minute presentation on your research, including key ideas, algorithms and results. Topics could include data modeling, extraction, search, natural language processing, document processing, handwriting recognition, machine learning, expert systems, social networks, human interfaces, data visualization, mobile technologies, automated research, cloud computing, security, or other areas of computing as they relate to family history research. Working demos are strongly encouraged. To submit an idea for a research talk, send a 1 - 2 page extended abstract, including citations, similar to what you would submit to a technical conference in your field of study. Include a short biographical statement and a link to your personal web presence and/or social media profile. ## How to Submit Presenters should submit the above information by January 15, 2016 to fhtw@internet.byu.edu. Submissions will be reviewed by members of the program committee. {% endfilter %}
